Papolapy Language

Mini Emotion Expression Language

English / Espanol / Francais / Русский

Want to learn how to express your emotions simply?

There's nothing easier, just remember a few simple rules:

  • To express your emotions and feelings, use the symbols of the Papolapy language in text, and the corresponding words in speech. This will make your message more expressive and emotionally rich.
  • It's best to place the symbol before the emotional phrase, but it's not required. You can decide for yourself where it fits best — there are no strict rules.
  • To express multiple emotions at once, simply place the symbols with a space between them, and pronounce the words with a pause. This will help convey the full depth of your feelings clearly.
Symbol Pronunciation Meaning
>p< papo Exclamation, attention call
>o< ohopo Agreement, approval
>i< imapo Negation, disagreement
>y< yono Surprise, fear
>f< fodo Extreme discontent, aggression
>l< lapy Joy, tenderness, interest in the topic

The Philosophy of Papolapy

Every conversation, whether online or in real life, is not just about exchanging information but also about conveying emotions. On the internet, we rely on emojis, but there are so many of them that it's easy to get confused, and they look different on each platform. In real life, we depend on facial expressions, but they don't always help either: not every feeling can be expressed with just one glance. So why not simplify things? Papolapy Language is a mini-language that makes expressing emotions easy, clear, and universal. Forget about complicated rules: just add symbols or words, and your emotions will shine in new colors! Papolapy is your key to the heart of your conversation partner.

Interesting Facts

  • Papolapy Language contains just 6 main words and symbols, making it one of the simplest languages in the world and, moreover, the easiest to remember.
  • Papolapy Language was created in 2012 and has been around for 12 years. Its popularity has varied over time, but it has been mentioned in various online communities, social media, and even in several stories. The total number of speakers of this language has long since surpassed 50,000.
  • The idea behind the Papolapy language was born out of a desire to unite people, regardless of their age, nationality, or habits.
  • In fact, Papolapy is a metalanguage that complements ordinary speech or text, making emotions more understandable. However, this doesn't stop it from being humorously referred to as a full-fledged language.
